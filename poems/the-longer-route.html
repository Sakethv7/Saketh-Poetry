Lyric Meditation

The Longer Route

A walk through memory, identity, and the self we carry home

I

I take a longer route as I walk

Past the street that I had forgotten

Laden with flowers it awaits

My treacherous mind's silence.

◆
II

Unspoken words seldom spill out

As tears that trickle down slow

Weighing a life's worth of sorrow

That will guide me astray tomorrow

◆
III

Smiling my way through old memories

That were left in upon my almira

It is a wonder that the delusion

Kept itself going without confusion.

◆
IV

Is it a memory or a longing —

The grassland, the sunflowers, and the blue sky

A child running through yellow light

Or a life I haven't lived into

◆
V

The mind plays its film without asking

My eyes well up and they look away

While I close the almira, call it nothing

Only to weep without warning one day

◆
VI

I wore the face that I led myself into

And somewhere lost the one beneath

My poetry bleeds at every turn now

For the self that got lost in time

◆
VII

Will she too see only what I've shown

And love a face who I've long outgrown

Or will she find what I haven't yet

A self I cannot remember or own

◆
VIII

Gradually, I remember the tears

The heart that withered at a friend's death

Lifetime worth of non validated memories

What is true and what is false anymore.

◆
IX

Various melodies arrive in a tongue I knew,

A Spiritual tune, another with my mother's voice

Another which reminisced in a love that left,

Or a tune for a love not yet born

◆
X

It carried an immense longing I cannot place in time

Perhaps carried from a life before this

I do not know if I need to be held

Or if my arms are meant to hold instead

◆
XI

I am now tired of finding answers

Loneliness is not the cause of this state

When the heart does not have refuge

As the call for help was never a smile.

◆
XII

Seasons change with every turn

Of my poetry and its passages

As I look at the serene sunset with wonder

Of when it will all dawn on my heart, again.

◆
XIII

I take a longer route as I drive

Through the lush green nature

Where the fresh air makes me bribe

My wounded soul, guiding itself into solace.

The road home is never the shortest one.
