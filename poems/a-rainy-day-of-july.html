A Rainy Day of July

Met on a rainy day of July — left on a day of autumn.

I

Did I ever wryly abide

To her nostalgic smile

That still lingers on this fray

That I wore on a rainy day.

◆
II

I saw a shocking reflection

By a puddle's misdirection

While I picked the fine rose

Laying beside my damp toes.

◆
III

It was a rainy day of July

When I met a woman so shy,

She had an umbrella that was blue

And a chilly wind had just blew.

◆
IV

Beauty personified her eyes

That made me conjure lies

About myself and the poem

That I read & write in tandem.

◆
V

My love was like a seagull

That crashed in a death fatal,

Yet she was the only twilight

That kindled the sparks inside.

◆
VI

I left her on a day of autumn

That I may regret often

As my love for her was hopeless

While she made me breathless.

◆
VII

Could it be luck or fate

To meet her on this lane

After many sorrowful seasons

That made me commit treasons.

◆
VIII

Treasons are the lines of poetry

Which I find along with this diary

That has her caged with grace

Like a flower in a rainy place.

◆
IX

It seems my love for her

Still sends the same shiver

That makes me want to cry

Like it was a rainy day of July.

She made me breathless — and my love for her was hopeless.
