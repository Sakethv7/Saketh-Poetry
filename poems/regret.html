Anaphoric Verse

Regret

The things I should have known.

I

If I had known he wouldn't laugh with me again,

I'd have held him once more — before the rain.

◆
II

If I hadn't taken him for granted,

I might have heard the quiet he hid from everyone.

◆
III

If I had understood that her kindness wasn't love,

I wouldn't have fallen so far into it.

◆
IV

If I had known this regret would arrive,

I'd have spoken. Just once, out loud.

◆
V

If my mind hadn't turned against me first,

I might have forgiven the losses before they settled.

◆
VI

If I had seen what her eyes were asking,

she wouldn't be something I still write around.

◆
VII

Long ago, I chose to feel everything too deeply.

I've seen, since then, things this mind can't unlearn.

◆
VIII

If I had known there was always a boat to solitude,

I would have gone quietly

instead of fighting every current home.

◆
IX

If I had known that betrayal was just

the shape a life takes —

I would have found a gentler way to let this one go.

The things I should have known.
