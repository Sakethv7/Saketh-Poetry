The Hairpin

Written for the ones who go to war

I

It's a lonely Saturday, I watch the jet planes fly into oblivion

Lying on my roof, I spectate as a memory burns away

With the cigarette, that burned beside me wailing as it lay.

◆
II

First view of the city is always different in the morning

I see it, the boy cries a little less as he gets flimsy

And the windows, of the old building seem a little less dusty.

◆
III

Shelter from the storm, I was given when I passed her way

It was another lifetime, when everything was new and bold

A transient memory, which is now old.

◆
IV

Not a word was spoken, the first time I met her gaze

The rain showered, heavily upon us on this old alleyway

I could hear piano strings, in my heart playing away.

◆
V

I hoped, that I won't fall in love with her

Since love, never fails to make me blue

And as the whistle blows, I boarded the train that was always due.

◆
VI

I bid her farewell, and as a tear fell from her cheek

The train started, moving in a motion swift

While I did not confess, these feelings that I had wrapped in a gift.

◆
VII

It was a hairpin, that she once was mesmerised by

Now it remains, with me forever hidden

As I had gone to war, for people to be ridden.

◆
VIII

When I came back, with an artificial eye

Which only sees the past, a time with her

And the other, sees a hairpin and jet planes since December.

A poem of war, return, and what the heart carries home.
