The Blue Scarf

She worked at a bookshop that played music for wanderers.

I

A wry memory alludes my mind

As I think about a straining rhyme

For a poem that is hard to write

While the world changes with time.

◆
II

Opposite to the bus stop's street

Was a bookshop laden with lily

And juniper that formed a sweet

Scenery in order with the reality.

◆
III

The shade of this old bus stop

Misses the serene glimpses of her,

Who worked at an artful bookshop

Which played music for the wanderer.

◆
IV

I used to wait for my bus on a floor

Until I noticed flowers that wither,

Through the window of that store

Which are still there, except her.

◆
V

She had a pashmina for the winter

And a light blue scarf to cover her

Hair that would make even summer

Shy & wary until it's September.

◆
VI

Her long hair flowed with the winds

That were chilly & cold in October

While the enigma of her red heels

Made me feel in peace, like a river.

◆
VII

I met her on a fateful rainy day

In this stop's magnanimous shade

As her eyes made my thoughts stray

While I hid my emotions in charade.

◆
VIII

It seemed as if she could clearly see

The wicked world & its foolish ways

From eyes that were blue like the sea

Yet I write a verse that only fakes.

◆
IX

It's been many seasons since I saw

Her in that empty book store

Which without her, is like a flaw

And the music fades with no flow.

◆
X

Therefore all I do is keep her

Alive in my melancholic memory,

As we met in the shade of a September

Who now lives on through my poetry.

A blue scarf in the wind — and a poetry that only preserves.
