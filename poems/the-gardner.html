The Gardner

Seasons passed. She kept coming. He never forgot.

I

A Gardner, lonely as he could be

Works at a garden in the city

Where flowers crave to be free

From the despondent calamity.

◆
II

A calamity that showers rain

Full of sorrow and regret

That causes a nostalgic pain

That he can never seem to forget.

◆
III

He observes the world changing,

But his pain that seems sweet

Abides, & also does the clinking

Of glass windows from the street.

◆
IV

He often notices the woman

Who lives in that old house

Where the glass clinks during dawn

And its a place without a spouse.

◆
V

Time and again she comes by

The garden for a flower lily,

Blooming in peace and solitary

And as sun dawns, she bids goodbye.

◆
VI

Her eyes looked in the garden

For something far ahead in life

That made him write a poem

About a woman who wasn't a wife.

◆
VII

In summer time, the woman

Would wear dark sunglasses

That didn't let any human

See her exuberant face.

◆
VIII

Spring season was their favourite

As she could see more beauty

In the garden while the literate

Gardener could do his duty.

◆
IX

On a rainy day, she would quietly

Stand with a blue umbrella,

Beside the flowers in the vicinity

While raindrops fell on his viyella.

◆
X

In autumn she would look

At the brown leaves that fall

While she clenched a book

With her hands that seemed small.

◆
XI

During winter, she would wear

A trench coat that was brown

And visit the garden lair

& In sorrow, they both would drown.

◆
XII

Numerous seasons passed

While she kept coming to the garden

For the flowers that weren't recast

As the man had never forgotten.

Seasons of a love that lived entirely in the garden — never spoken.
