The Bar Under a Lonely Star

Only broken people gathered there — just to get a sip of whiskey & lime.

I

I stumbled upon a secluded bar

One breezy & windy December day.

It was situated under a lonely star

But, was filled with people anyway.

◆
II

Here only broken people gathered

Just to get a sip of whiskey & lime

That has long since been withered

On these long days with no sunshine.

◆
III

I entered with no specific objective

But this peculiar place was on its own

Tied to a premise in an anxious subjective

Tone, only to get hopelessly drunk alone.

◆
IV

A gloomy shadow looms over

An old man's scar filled face,

A face that hadn't come to lower

Its pride which now, seems a charade.

◆
V

A semi shattered vinyl player

Grinds through the gloomy night

And the lonesome could only savour

A sharp tonic with a lonesome plight.

◆
VI

Wistful smiles popped on the buzzed

As a specific melody reminded them

Of a time when the heart wasn't puzzled,

And of a time when life was a poem.

◆
VII

A trumpet blares through the shade

Stirring a melancholic sensation

And the piano artfully lets the pain fade

While it dissolves in the mind's ocean.

◆
VIII

The sounds of distant glasses clink

Carried by a sadness stuck on their face

Made my empty and horrid heart sink

In misery as a dire mockery was in place.

◆
IX

And as I sit down by this lonely lady

Whose main motive in life was only to

Measure her costly glass of brandy

At the bar where the bill stayed due.

◆
X

My gutted face enveloped in sorrow

My pale and dry lips subtly drown

In the glass of whiskey too shallow

While my hands tremble to a sound.

◆
XI

A sound of a woman who is beside me,

Forcing me to hopelessly remember

A time with a woman who was like a maple tree,

Who blew away with the winds of December.

◆
XII

This woman beside me was dressed in blue,

And tried to see the scribbling in my diary

But a cold wind came in and hastily blew

Away the poems that described a canary.

◆
XIII

Before midnight, I exited from this bar

Which almost made me its abductee.

Now in the night sky, I see a lonely star

That others inside the bar couldn't see.

◆
XIV

I light up a cigarette as I stand below

A streetlight that illuminates the dark

Streets of this town covered in snow

And the rain while I decide to embark,

◆
XV

On a journey to find the correct word

To complete this rigid poem of mine.

I smoke while I mark a grave for a bird

Which lied inside my lonely & rusty heart.

◆
XVI

It died without seeing a ray of sunshine

That escapes from my diary, but I can't save

These poems in my diary forever, that act as light

For the flowers to bloom at my isolated grave.

◆
XVII

Time has hurried on, as I kept on writing

These miserable poems, that have me and her.

 

While she blew away with the cold winds

Of December, that have now, run full circle.

A dead bird in his heart — and the lonely star outside that others couldn't see.
