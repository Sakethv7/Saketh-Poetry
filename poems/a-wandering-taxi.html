A Wandering Taxi

The city at midnight.

A wandering taxi stopped in the corner of my street, I sat inside and told him to drive till dawn. His dejected face and his eyes made me realise that we both were wanderers that were just waiting for their moment to drown.

Dawdles, the midnight.

Lonely streets vanish in the wind as the taxi moves, leaving us with lonely faces, lonely hearts and some remorseful memories. While the taxi swerves through the city, tears fall down my face as I reminisce a time when my heart had an empty room, before she barged in and filled it with a wound deep enough.

Lingers on, this wound.

I fell in love with her the day I met her, it felt tragic as I couldn't keep my eyes off her. I saw her in class, seated beside a guy, holding hands with him while my pen fell in disbelief as I could see my fate recur.

Dawdles on, your memory.

Questioning the cruelty of my fate, I spent my days in absolute misery. How could it be that one day life is bliss and the next day, a regretful mockery.

Laughs, my fate.

Being her friend somedays felt joyful but often it was a saddening anecdote which on every recollection, became darker. My heart desired to stay beside her even if it didn't mount to anything yet it was a torture to be someone of no significance to her.

Dithers on, my desire.

My heart thought of her as someone that it could have but never keep. It felt like a sin, to have feelings for her when it wouldn't reach her even if it had taken a faithful leap.

Loiters on, my sin.

When this heart had realised the truth, life swept through in a blur. She is about to be married today and this heart can just hum a saddening melody to soothe my soul until one day, the sun shines brighter.

I cling onto, my heart's lie.

The taxi stops at the same place I boarded it from. Faint sunshine seeps through my coat and this cold as I walk to the coffee shop where I first saw those brown eyes and fell for them.

Linger on, your pale brown eyes.

The city at midnight.
