I've Known Life

I've known only one thing that flows through everything.

I

My blurred and teary sight

Watches the leaves of a tree

Dance to the tunes of the rain

While I write a poem about a bee.

◆
II

I watch as the rain drops pour

On my desolated shade,

I reminisce a time that bore

Sorrows in my life's charade.

◆
III

As the lush green leaves fall

Reminding me the fall of twenty

When a love stayed unrequited

From a joyful & hopeful reality.

◆
IV

These chilly and cold winds

Write a melancholic story

About the grieve stricken fields

In the broken hearted diary.

◆
V

My watch is soaked in the rain

And raindrops fall & slide down

The watch while I smoke in vain

Wishing if that could ever change.

◆
VI

My rusty bicycle is just for show

It reminds me of my childhood

When life was just too slow

And time was spent for good.

◆
VII

Cold winds accompany the rains

While people just don't mind

How the lonely world feigns

A joy which is only found inside.

◆
VIII

A sorrowful night is received

As the day ends and the rain

Is visible only under the street

Lights that show a way again.

◆
IX

Now as the lonesome hours

And chilly minutes pass by,

I have known only one thing

That flows through everything,

◆
X

I've known life.

I've known life.
