Painting Her

She smiled over a novel while I drew her reflection on a wine bottle.

I

A railroad rusted in no mystery

Is beside a small coffee house,

I try to paint a vivid scenery

Dusted by life without a spouse.

◆
II

In my quiet longing for the spring

I see a woman walk by in the rain

While flowers bloom in the morning

That were withered for long in pain.

◆
III

The winds of the rain dance

Around her which reminds me

Of a sparkling coast in Barcelona

Where the sun is calm as the sea.

◆
IV

Sipping the coffee while reading

An obscure romance novel,

She smiles while I keep painting

Her reflection I see on a wine bottle.

◆
V

Losing track of weather & season

I bide my time for a new artistry,

To draw her eyes for no reason

On my canvas under an oak tree.

◆
VI

When the evening comes in haste

I smoke a cigarette of mint

And like the clouds in Autumn

She disappears without a hint.

◆
VII

As the days of snow approached

She stopped visiting this place,

Making the memories a facade

And turning my art into a daze.

◆
VIII

A painting with a lonely memory

Is on my canvas as if it was a fantasy.

It's an art that awaits a melody

Which danced around her beauty.

◆
IX

The season of spring nears me

And my loneliness is on a spree

Of painting her reminiscent imagery

That is etched into my heart for eternity.

A painting that awaits a melody — which danced around her beauty.
