The Café in March

What a café window reveals.

I

I cross puddles formed by

the drizzle of rain in March

which I often foolishly pry

on this lane and in my scotch.

◆
II

I hear the faint chirping

of birds while I saunter

by a garden, pondering

about feelings that linger.

◆
III

Peculiar thoughts chased

my mind while I sipped coffee

in a café as I remained dazed

by a woman who chose to flee,

 

from my heart and my poem

that remains wry and incomplete

to this day as my pen in tandem

has yet to live up to its conceit.

◆
IV

I see the world ever changing

while I stay in this café's shade

taking my time and observing

how with life, my emotions fade.

◆
V

I notice a mother without a ring

who has two kids by her side,

frantic to share her loneliness

but is clueless where to confide.

◆
VI

I spotted an old man drown

in his sorrows and in his drink

while his shirt that was brown

had seen grief without a blink.

◆
VII

I caught a glimpse of a woman

who has eyes that were blue

drank a juice that had lemon

and had an umbrella that was blue.

◆
VIII

My teary eyes await the rain

as only my rhymes get to know

and see me cry in the nights of pain

while life just seems to flow.

 

Rain in the midst of March, made

me see a scene that has shades

and are now etched under this shade

as I write about how it all fades.

What a café window reveals.
