Simple Twist of Fate

Wild strawberry fields and a missed bus.

I

Half drunk and completely hopeless,

I keep chiming on this typewriter

while a hummingbird hums melancholy

through these lonesome nights.

◆
II

The morning rises with a grief I can't name —

I only know it started the day I saw her smile

and mistook it for a door.

◆
III

I missed my bus on a lonesome rainy day.

Life changed with a simple twist of fate:

she was standing beside the wild strawberry fields

under that gloomy shade,

and I forgot where I was going.

◆
IV

I was guilty of daydreaming in her eyes,

in the little details of her life,

while time slipped through without asking.

Now even the clock is unbearable —

it only shows how slowly things move

when you are waiting for nothing.

◆
V

She showed me winter could have colour.

She was proof that autumn could break your heart

while still being beautiful.

 

I wonder if I'll find her again —

near the garden full of lilies,

at the old bookstore,

or under an umbrella.

◆
VI

The words still fall on this page,

and I keep writing,

half drunk,

completely hopeless,

waiting one more time

for a simple twist of fate.

Wild strawberry fields and a missed bus.
