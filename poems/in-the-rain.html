In the Rain

For a friend lost to the rain.

I

This rain that pours me with sorrow and grief

also took away my world's only relief.

My friend, who smiled brightly even when the nights felt shallow,

left his loved ones and this world which had made him hollow.

So many days have slipped by

but this agony doesn't pass by.

This distress often returns in my life,

like the rain which doesn't know when to confide.

◆
II

In just two years, we had found a brother in each other,

but we never found closure for the crosses we had borne for the other.

While I never realised how greatly I would regret

for not helping you when you were in need,

I went on with my life with this anguish in my heart which never deceased.

I walk with regret on this life's slope,

and I stroll on this lonely road which has fell silent

without your voice filled with hope.

They remind me of all those memories that now seem bittersweet,

of us having a laugh about how life had its fair share of sour and sweet.

◆
III

Now as I move on with my life, I carry a sheet filled with poems,

about a friend whom I lost amidst the rain filled with regrets and sorrows.

So as I move on in life and write these poems

under the roof of an old soul who looks to pay his amend,

thus I could only hope

that you would think about me every now and then,

old friend.

For a friend lost to the rain.
