The Tattered Page

Written near a lake's shore, the night before the front.

I

A soldier writes on a tattered page,

A poem written near a lake's shore

That is outside the tents of shade,

As of tomorrow, he heads for war.

◆
II

It's a lonely & desperate cry for help

Under the starry filled night sky

He writes this poem on the things he felt

About the incidents he perceived in July

◆
III

On duty, so he may not forget reality.

◆
IV

He saw a fright in the eyes of a recruit

Rushing towards the sole bunker

Where resides a major who is a hoot,

A man who will follow death in a flutter.

◆
V

Hearts that had forgotten to slow down

Kept running in a dire & constant horror

As a bomb on the battlefield may drop down,

Adding more bodies to become fodder.

◆
VI

He saw a bunch of pilots meeting

Their families, the smiles were cut in

With tears of sorrow that were falling,

As of tomorrow, they are airborne therein.

◆
VII

He heard the story of the lieutenant

Who was in enemy land without a dorm,

A woman who lived near as a tenant

Gave him a shelter from the storm.

◆
VIII

The woman had silver bracelets

In her wrists & flowers in her hair

She died in his arms, as bullets fired in sets,

Now he has no heart for his own share.

◆
IX

He observes a deputy who had erupted

In anger at the sepoys who were afraid

Of the corpses which fate had decided,

And God had made flowers in facade.

◆
X

The soldier gets up the next day

He arms himself to feel a bit secure

While the army doesn't intend to delay,

As they join the frontlines to endure.

◆
XI

All of them were given a deadly boot

As everyone was killed in a dogfight.

He was wounded & wanted to shoot

Himself to be relieved of the spite.

◆
XII

As he was about to push the gun's trigger

He sights a thorny rose beside his feet

On a pool of blood that made him shiver,

He picked it & under a tree, he found a seat.

◆
XIII

A seat beside the pile of corpses

That were forming a haunting shadow.

Grief, misery & sorrow now courses

Through his veins that are now hollow.

◆
XIV

He had a lot of blood loss from the wound

In his arm, yet he hangs onto the rose

Firmly, while he also clutches the shroud

Of his comrade, who died without a repose.

◆
XV

The thorny rose stays with & by him

Until he breathes his last breath,

He wonders if someone read the poem

He wrote near that shore as now he is dead.

◆
XVI

He died under the lonely shade of a tree

While a wind blows, making the leaves fall

On his body, that once was on a kill spree,

As nature marks a grave for him, this fall.

◆
XVII

The battlefield had become a graveyard

Without anyone's name. Corpses lie in wait,

While a silent wind blows through the yard,

As it's September, a month that they hate.

A poem read by no one — and a soldier who will never know.
