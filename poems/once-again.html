Anaphoric Verse

Once Again

The cyclical nature of longing.

I

Once again,

the regrets keep burdening my shoulder

while the melodies which once offered me joy

now remind me of a pain that tries to linger

and of a sorrow that this pen does enjoy.

◆
II

One more time,

I wanted myself to stay

but my mind advised constraint

as my tears no longer lay

in wait for her, while my eyes can only refrain.

◆
III

How can I erase these,

memories that only bring tears

of sorrow and benign

and will continue for years

unless I heal this broken heart of mine.

◆
IV

One last time,

I want to see her somewhere

at the same old coffee house that still is plain

in a bookstore, reading the sonnets of yesteryear

or in a shade, staying away from rain.

◆
V

And in those moments,

will I be able to find closure?

over this love that is deemed to be hopeless

will my words of love reach her?

that fill my poems, yet, feel wordless.

◆
VI

Once again,

my conscience threatens to be horrified

as it seems that these words might fall short

before reaching her, while I abide

to a twist of fate, that will retort.

◆
VII

I wait for the day,

when this mind may numb this pain

that comes and goes in pieces that stray

as only the memories remain

while I hope that the sorrow slowly fades away.

◆
VIII

These poems stare at me for hours,

I write these poems for her

or for a woman that seems to be her

to read these poems, is all I want from her

while I wait for a reply from her.

 

Once again I wait,

for a woman to walk into this room

in my heart that has long been closed

and I smoke a cigarette in wait, that doesn't seem to be a boon

while the dying embers of this cigarette that has dozed

and of my love,

 

fall on this poem.

The cyclical nature of longing.
