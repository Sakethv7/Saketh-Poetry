September

A month that never intends to stay.

I

She was a woman of my dreams

who was like the ending of summer

and now I have wasted years

in pursuit of forgetting her.

◆
II

A woman who appeared in a storm

came and instilled a calmness

that I couldn't have asked as my norm

yet, it's all I could ask in a tone, reckless.

◆
III

She was a woman who brought spring

into my rain and sorrow filled life

but she disappeared in the wind

just when my love had come alive.

◆
IV

She was a woman whose smile

had become my heart's desire

and as I dreamt her on an aisle

she faded and made my life a satire.

◆
V

She seldom made me question

my sentimental approach to destiny

now all I do is smile in tandem

with the poems I write in mockery.

◆
VI

Her hands so warm and kind

played the piano in the key of C

that brought solace to my mind

while I wrote a haiku that got lost in sea.

◆
VII

She brought a change in me for good

while I wanted to be close to her

yet I could never get the chance

as she always had him beside her.

◆
VIII

Perhaps she's the only reason

why I write this sorrowful poem

however, loving her maybe my treason

as I have forgotten the song she did hum.

◆
IX

Maybe the reason I write this song

is a way of avenging my grief

but her memory does prolong

a sorrow that I have hid in this leaf.

◆
X

Now as I sit on this park bench

I reminisce a time of August

while the leaves that fall on the trench

have turned to brown in downcast.

◆
XI

I take a trip down memory lane

when I had bid farewell to my love

inspite of my mind's constant refrain

as my heart had given up on its luck.

◆
XII

My heart is trying to rejoin in trust

with leaves of spring that were green

and now have turned to brown this August

while I just get old with poems in between.

◆
XIII

As seasons have passed quickly

I started questioning my empathy

that seems to have flown swiftly

in this wind that flows until its rainy.

◆
XIV

I have never been in love before

is what my paranoid peers often say

but my poems act as proof on their own

as these people tend to be in dismay.

◆
XV

She was the woman I met in September,

a month that never intends to stay.

A month that never intends to stay.
