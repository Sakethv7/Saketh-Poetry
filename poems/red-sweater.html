Red Sweater

Written under the shade of a garden

I

The sun shines less than usual

Like a lost love finding itself in a gravel,

The fallen leaves do not appear as lovely

An autumn's sign for travel.

◆
II

I sit under the shade of this garden

Penning my thoughts down that take shape

Of poetry that remains too brazen

For my heart has lost an escape.

◆
III

A sudden gush of wind flows past my book

Flowers of the garden dance to its tune

While I smoke away my feelings that shook

Me and my poetry on a fateful day of June.

◆
IV

Thoughts become fleeting under this tree

Memories that once sparked an ember

In my heart still lurks in my poetry

Keeping me warm in this lonely winter.

◆
V

She often wore a red sweater,

Calling me when she wanted attention

Texting me to get her man under the weather

Making me join my own pitiful convention.

◆
VI

The birds fly from one place to another

Reminding me her words of that night

When she cried a river, and now I ponder

With all the time that has passed by, will she think of me tonight?

◆
VII

My friends tell me to quit smoking

But it's these memories that haunt my fingers

Which grips the cigarette, burning

The sorrows inside my heart that to this day, lingers.

◆
VIII

Like a cigarette that breaks in the rain

I break my heart every time I see this deluge

It hides my seemingly shallow pain

And under this shade my pen takes a silent refuge.

An autumn poem — seasons and sorrows that linger.
