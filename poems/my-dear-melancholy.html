My Dear Melancholy

A dialogue with sorrow itself

I

O my dear melancholy

You just took her face

Caressing the green grass

You have arrived so late.

◆
II

The old letters make me ponder

If the pen writes through its own will

Slipping emotions through a finger

Putting me in a saddening bliss.

◆
III

In a quiet tide of ignorance

A past joy that was fleeting

Now weighs down heavily

On my cigarette that keeps breaking.

◆
IV

I shadow the joy that seeps

Through the merry day

Wanting to touch me heaps

But, you, never let it find a way.

◆
V

You quietly dissect every feud

Carving your way with glee

And I never end up finding what I need

But you find your way to me.

◆
VI

O my dear melancholy

How much have I missed you?

O my dear melancholy

When will I forsake you?

Where sadness becomes a companion, not a curse.
