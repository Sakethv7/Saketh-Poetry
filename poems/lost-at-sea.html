Lost at Sea

The helmsman's grief.

I

Lost at sea,

the horizon as the captain's destination

a memory in his mind starts to flee

reminding him of a time when he was free from his desolation.

◆
II

Even with burdens on their shoulders

the passengers seem to smile freely

yet a misery resides on their fingers

while the helmsman steers through the waves cautiously.

◆
III

Ponders the yachtsman with a heart that is heavy

and a soul that can never be free

from all the times he drinks in misery

and the times when this heart was his own and once, so was she.

◆
IV

With an aching in their heart

the dockers dance in the early mornin rain

the seafarer watches on, near a pillar that is scarred

and with eyes that reflect his long lost pain.

◆
V

The old salt remembers a place where the liquor tasted good

now, in the sea, it tastes of his guilt, anger

and a depression that he has withstood

while he searches for a rope that feels rougher.

◆
VI

This old boat gets the bosun down, it makes him suffer

as a rush of distasteful memories come to his mind

pushing him to his limits and making him tougher

all he does is cling on to a hope, a hope that one day he could go see his child.

◆
VII

The ferryman feels stuck on this boat

feeling cold, drunk and lethargic

he rows with a might that lets it stay afloat

and now only God could bring him hope.

◆
VIII

Somedays a blue mist surrounds the sailor's soul

he wonders when his heart would be free

from the sorrow that makes him whole

and from the apathy that binds his heart from being carefree.

◆
IX

At the end of the sea

the cap'n stops searching for a purpose or a destination

he realises that he can't see

the horizon that he once felt was his salvation.

◆
X

The company grasp their life's lonely tale

as they come to understand

that life is all about the journey in detail

rather than its destination, that once had made them leave their homeland.

The helmsman's grief.
