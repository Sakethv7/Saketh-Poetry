The Woman Who Brought Spring

She returned the season.

I

I wandered in a garden full of marigold

It reminded me of a time that has flown

When romantic poetry was for the old

And for the people who were left alone.

◆
II

I saw a sage like alyssum flower

Hiding in beauty among others,

It resembled a girl I met in October

When leaves fall like feathers.

◆
III

These flowers that are not cherished

Wither and die like the autumn leaves

Which are colored in a lonely dismay

Waiting to turn callous and grey.

◆
IV

My wish to watch the crooked rain

Again with her by my side felt a fantasy.

And when droplets slid down this shade

I saw her again in all her beauty.

◆
V

She entered this shade by the flowers

And the birds sang the tale of her beauty

In a nostalgic & melancholic symphony

Which pierced the lines in my poetry.

◆
VI

She was a woman who I knew once

Her smile could even make a rose shy

A smile that made me fall in love

A love which was new but now is old.

◆
VII

The rain falling on this shade felt serene

But her nostalgic smile like the flowers

Seemed more calmer than the rain

Which fatefully made us meet again.

◆
VIII

Greetings were exchanged, like the day

We met in this garden seasons ago,

Now she smiles with a few wrinkles

And my words still stumble to flow.

◆
IX

I saw her kids stroll along the garden

A bittersweet sight for a rainy day

A wry smile appeared on the curtain

Of my heart that reflected on my face.

◆
X

She bid farewell as the rain subsided

Her kids walked under an umbrella

Which a long time ago, I had gifted,

And I was left longing for the rain again.

◆
XI

I sighed as a snowy winter had arrived

The chilly winds entered this shade

Bringing forth emotions this December

That put an end to the reminiscence of her.

◆
XII

A beautiful reminiscence that once

Was present in my heart for a lifetime

Now is left aside while I wander again

Without a purpose, writing down

◆
XIII

Poetry that does not have her in it,

And a mystery that eludes me

Seeps into my mind as I look down

To the snow swept streets

◆
XIV

Realising, that life in all its misery

Just goes on.

She returned and so did the season.
