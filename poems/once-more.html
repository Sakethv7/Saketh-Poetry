Once More

On love that returns to haunt.

I

I had forgotten her voice and face,

but now I can see her in the flower in my vase.

Now she messages me again,

making me reminisce the love I had to refrain.

◆
II

The serenity in her voice and eyes,

yet again brings me to my knees at sunrise.

She showers her signs of sympathy upon me,

feels like an utter and unjust misery.

◆
III

When my hands had just found peace,

she comes again to rattle my sleep.

My hands have started wandering again,

towards a pen that writes down my pain.

◆
IV

Now I write down a poem about a tale,

that seems to her like a funny charade.

Golden slumbers fill her eyes and dry mine,

a sorrow that befalls on my words, on this line.

◆
V

I was on a hiatus from this suffering,

now she returns with a smile that seems to be a good way of covering.

But this heart knows that it's a comedy,

for my soul knows about her apathy.

◆
VI

I had met her and that was fate's twist,

but now, she comes back with a cynical fit.

I write beside a window on a rainy day,

and these words strain to rhyme yet, I pray.

◆
VII

Now I move on with my life as I did before,

without any concrete purpose anymore.

I must keep on writing poems about her and someone like her,

until I find the words to this incomplete poem,

that lies ahead on this long and winding road.

On love that returns to haunt.
