Homeward Bound

Going back to a city that no one calls home.

I

This car won't slow down in dim light

Even with the heavy riverside wind

Pushing against it with a might

Which makes it seem like a headwind.

◆
II

The winds blow here ferociously

Along these country roads with a sunshine

That caresses my heavy heart tenderly

Beside these fields of Juniper & Alpine.

◆
III

A nostalgic memory races my mind's lair

As the highway wind hits my face

And brushes through my messy hair

While sun shines brightly on my charade.

◆
IV

I recall the times when fruits were collected

From the lakeside farms that were near

Our place where everyone gathered

And came to the country home I hold dear.

◆
V

Grandparents, uncles, aunts, cousins,

Every one of them visited to enjoy

The cool summer breeze, that now

Blows with an empty ahoy

As time slipped through & didn't allow,

◆
VI

Me to even bid farewell to the people

I held in great regard and affection,

Now I stand in these fields that has a beetle

Climbing on a branch with no conviction.

◆
VII

The beetle reminded me of myself

A kid who's ignorance was bliss

Now I see is a world that has scared itself

From words that feign love in distress.

◆
VIII

Confabulating with the people around

I spent my summer days there in solace

Now I am bound to my city with a frown

On my face & with boots without shoelace.

◆
IX

The old people that once had stayed

Already died in a simple twist of fate.

So I had visited today, that tragic shade

To pay respects in silence while I did wait.

◆
X

With feelings overwhelming to the core

I write this poem as I travel to the airport

With stuff that I bought from the store

Where people smiled to heal scars in rapport.

◆
XI

I wish to be homeward bound in reality

Except, I am going to a place

That I can hardly call a home in fidelity

So I write this verse with a mask on my face.

◆
XII

The place I go back to with a frown

Has no one to even call my own

Hypocrites, surround me as I drown

In the sea of words that I have never known.

◆
XIII

No lover waits for me back in the city

The one whom I loved long ago had left

And now this place that I leave is empty

Except for a blue bird that lies with its nest.

◆
XIV

Never could I see the stars in the city

Yet to write beneath the diamond sky

In that country town in tranquility

Is how I would like my nights to go by.

◆
XV

The town has flowers glittered

That never bend in the rainfall

But now the flowers in the city have withered

As there hasn't been sunshine since fall.

◆
XVI

All these places have their own memories

Well it be the city or that town since years,

Yet I enjoyed my days in the windy breeze

Of my childhood place that I leave in tears.

◆
XVII

Though I know I will never lose a love innate

For people and things that went before

But I know that they will stay in my shade

As they are in my poems that I still explore.

◆
XVIII

These memories will stay in this poem

And I will continue to pretend that

These memories will remain in tandem

As I know I'll often think about those,

 

Flowers that never bend with the rainfall,

 

And the wind that blows through the countryside.

The flowers that never bend with the rainfall — and a wind that blows through the countryside.
