Tale of the Wandering Poet

A meditation on versions and transformation

I

Which version is it?

I can't seem to find you in my poetry anymore.

Is it you or someone else?

In the years that I knew you,

now more years went past loving you.

Can I count it on my hand,

or the pages of poetry

that have you as a "her"

who just slips past as thoughts

from my heart through my fingers

into my diary's fading ink?

◆
II

Now, with your mention in its stead,

the page seems complete yet haunted

of the old memories that I let go.

But it does come back,

coming through the faint cracks

that slowly close as time passes by.

◆
III

Which version are you now,

that has bled into my heart today?

Which version are you tomorrow,

that sleeps silently as I prattle

about my life that is without worries?

◆
IV

Which version would you be

when I love someone again?

I am confident that this love,

that I used to feel for you,

will only make me feel even stronger

for the destined woman

that I have been waiting

for the past few lifetimes.

◆
V

Which version would you be, my destiny?

I await your soft advent into my heart

to win it without contention.

◆
VI

Which version am I?

Living as a wandering poet,

believing, as I walk away from yesterday.

An interrogation of identity through the lens of lost love.
