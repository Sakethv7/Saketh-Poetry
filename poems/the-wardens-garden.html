The Warden's Garden

He kept the garden. The garden kept him.

I

A man with a horrific past

Had done unspeakable deeds

Now longs to find peace & be free

From those shackles of his creeds.

◆
II

He finds a work to escape from calamity

A place he could find the peace he craved

A place where people were like his family

A garden where his life felt paved.

◆
III

Spending his days by watering the garden

He thinks of himself as a lonely convict

And acts as a peaceful warden

While he smokes away his mind's verdict.

◆
IV

He meets a woman in that place

She was a maid, who carried a vase

And had a peculiar scar on her face

That reminded him of cruelty in solace.

◆
V

He gives sage advice to people around

Which he wished he had got in the past

While she stands in the corner of the room

With a smile that made him aghast.

◆
VI

He sits beside her on a rainy evening

As she knits him a glove for the winter

He looks at her eyes like the moon

And fears that he may fall in love with her.

◆
VII

A notice came from the army one evening

She opens the letter and gets tears

He finds out that he had been drafted

For the rest of his years.

◆
VIII

He had bought her a beautiful hair clip

Which he wanted to give her in repose

But now, he hides it in his room with a slip

Along with the feelings that he cannot disclose.

◆
IX

A wistful smile appears on his face

As he looks at what he leaves behind,

A room full of memories, a charming vase,

And people who were family to him.

◆
X

He heads to the station with only a bag full of memories

While the household cries in agony.

She grabs ahold of him just as he boards

Tears fall from her eyes in misery.

◆
XI

He wipes her tears, he asks her to marry

A nice man & wishes a good life to her.

No words of love were said in a hurry

As the eyes had already confessed, on one rainy September.

◆
XII

With a wry smile, he bids them farewell

The train departs, leaving only love

And solitude behind as fate could compel

He looks at her photo & could only dwell.

◆
XIII

He died in war, that was theirs to lose

And people left behind could just grieve

While a sorrow with an endless premise

Had landed on their heart's retrieve.

◆
XIV

She is now a Nana & has lived a happy life.

Now on her deathbed, she has only one regret,

She couldn't convey her feelings that night

To an old love that she could never forget.

◆
XV

She lost a love in this life's trip

While he just vanished in the wind

Leaving only that hair clip

And those lingering feelings behind,

◆
XVI

In that dusty room from ages ago

And the leaves that were green turn to brown.

A love confessed only by eyes — on one rainy September.
