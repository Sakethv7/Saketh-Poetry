Jessie

For the syllables I couldn't think of in a poem.

I

It's been a long time since I last heard you sing. Somedays I just find myself looking at your photograph while my heart just grieves.

◆
II

Now you have grown your hair and moved somewhere, a place where I could never come close to. I arrived at that place yet I remained distanced, a shimmering thought stopped me, making me blue.

◆
III

You never owed me anything, yet I owe everything I know about love and life, to you.

◆
IV

You were the syllables I couldn't think of in a poem and you were the melody that lacked in my symphony.

◆
V

I visualise you in my poetry, dancing with words in the garden of my heart while I aimlessly await your return to these rain drenched streets that are empty.

◆
VI

In my mind, you float across my lonely and grieving waters. With just a glimpse of your smile, my mind dispels all my horrors.

◆
VII

Some seasons ago, I read you in some kind of a poem. I shivered when I wondered how poets had come across love that felt like anguish for me and for them.

◆
VIII

I played my guitar one lonely evening, it wept through a soothing melody that felt blue, a tune that reminded me of how ill fated I was for not being with you.

◆
IX

A poem over you often feels undone. Then I wonder why I spend my time, writing poems that I can't believe, writing words that resemble you yet feel ridiculous when they rhyme.

◆
X

If she could read my mind, she could see a tale unfolding itself, a tale that lets me visualise her, standing still under the moonlight in Vermont.

◆
XI

When I can not sing my heart, I can only speak my mind. Then I write on these meaningless sheets, filling my heart out and I let the words sing a melody, a melody that has faint reminisces of you.

◆
XII

I may forget to write once I get old, I may forget the lies and the truths of my present and past, but the only truth worth remembering, would be you.

◆
XIII

Half of what I said is meaningless, but I said it just to reach you. Several seasons have passed and I couldn't reach you, so I write this poem of love for you, Jessie.

For the syllables I couldn't think of in a poem.
