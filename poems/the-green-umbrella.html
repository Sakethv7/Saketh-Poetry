The Green Umbrella

She walked away under the umbrella I had given her.

I

It's a lonely month of June

A windless summer time

I write with no rhythm or tune

As no rainy days are in sight.

◆
II

But this season ushered

A sudden rain in delight

In the heart that shattered

Several seasons ago in plight.

◆
III

This night chooses a dream

That was serene in my eyes,

How could I ever forget that smile

That resembled a night without lies.

◆
IV

I saw her near the old bus stop

Searching shelter from the rain,

I was beside the dusty shop

Soaking in a nostalgic pain.

◆
V

I gave her the green umbrella

Which she once had gifted me.

Now she grips the same Viyella

With a face full of glee.

◆
VI

As she walked beside me

The rainswept streets fell silent

And the winds played a memory

Which made my heart heavy.

◆
VII

I saw a ring on her finger

As she was picking a lily,

I felt a sorrow that will linger

In my poems of chivalry.

◆
VIII

Her hair reflected the moonlight

When she caught the bus in a hurry,

We bid an emotionless goodbye

While the night became hazy.

◆
IX

Now it's the month of July

The night chooses a dream

Where an old love chose to fly

But I kept mine, in a poem.

I kept mine, in a poem.
