After Many Seasons

Reunion and acceptance

I

I woke up remembering her face

A face that beamed with elegance

While life goes on without a trace

My soul now, awaits her embrace.

◆
II

I caught a glimpse of her strolling on a winter day

The cold flowers received her hand's warmth

A string of words caught at my diary's bay

Hopeless words that are filled with thorns.

◆
III

But an old regret came in like it was there

Waiting by the shore of my mind

A ghost that lingered in the salt air

Reminding me that life isn't always kind.

◆
IV

She sauntered across the garden's bridge

As if elegance was personified by her,

All I can do is pray that I get a chance

To tell her what I couldn't since that cold December.

◆
V

Her shadow falls on the fallen leaves

And as she passes by the dull daffodil

The feelingless garden now grieves

While I fail to conjure any syllable.

◆
VI

Our eyes met again on this fateful day

My heart stopped as she turned towards me

Her smile is as beautiful as the night we met in a cafe

While I just stood there thinking it wasn't to be.

◆
VII

After many seasons my umbrella found a companion,

Prattling about the good ol' days

We strolled through the sanctuary

And I pondered if eternity could feel this way.

◆
VIII

When we strolled at the end of the place

She bid me farewell at the bus stop in the rain

Leaving me alone in the garden of words

Making me hope that one day we could meet again.

◆
IX

She was a woman I once knew

Like a jasmine she poises elegantly

Even when it rains on days that are blue

While I try to move on through the lines of my poetry.

Where seasons turn, umbrellas find companions, and hope blooms again.
