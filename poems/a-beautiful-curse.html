A Beautiful Curse

An oak tree. Unanticipated rain. A love that only blooms in verse.

I

A beautiful autumn night sky

Enveloped a rather desolating

Conversation which seemed shy

While a nightingale was in hiding.

◆
II

An oak tree on top of a hill

Had leaves of colour blue

That fell & left a trail in shrill

While a chilly & cold wind blew.

◆
III

The changes in the seasons

Made her nonchalantly smile

& they faded swiftly with treasons

Of my past, that still hide in a lie.

◆
IV

A glimmer of lightning fazed

Us as we stood in the shade

Of an oak tree that was hazed

By the autumn's lie in charade.

◆
V

Unanticipated rain poured

On my already anxious heart

And on the tree that ignored

This life which has kept us apart.

◆
VI

I never tended to care about

The seasonal changes around me

But when I read a poem in doubt

My love for her bloomed like a lily.

◆
VII

Various seasons had passed

And in grief we diverged our path

While only memories could last

As now I sit under a shed of lath.

◆
VIII

This thin shed hasn't provided

Shade from the rain as I write

About a love that has subsided

As I await a nostalgic night.

◆
IX

I stumbled upon the old oak tree

Whose blue leaves fall in autumn

This night sky frees me from misery

And a memory seeps in my poetry.

◆
X

Poetry that alludes me everyday

Has been about a seasonal woman

That dances by a garden where I lay

Dreaming under the oak tree, in autumn.

A seasonal woman — and a love that is fatal every autumn.
