Narrative Verse

Tokyo

Two strangers in a city that has no gleeful clue

I

The red light shines brighter than usual

Suggesting a momentary stop.

The man looks for clues in the new city

An unknown place for poets to rot.

◆
II

Everything seems unsure and unheard

Most of his time is lost in translation.

He saunters through the streets

To find a bookstore, secluded from all the fabrication.

◆
III

He picks up a dusty novel that was light

As his old fashioned pack of cigarettes.

Reading a few pages of the old book

Reminds him of the thoughts that he cannot digest.

◆
IV

Sitting in the bar, surrounded by dim lights

He sips his whiskey and catches a glimpse or two,

Of a woman who wore herself a dress

Whose eyes read everything in the room and were blue.

◆
V

They somehow light up their cigarettes

And prattle about how they are lost

In this city which has no gleeful clue

About their sorrowful life, in short.

◆
VI

They roam the night in the streets of Tokyo

Finding moments of solace in clamour.

She dances like there's no tomorrow

And he just observes her undisputed glamour.

◆
VII

Now they lay on the insomniac bed

Talking about all the uncertainty

That looms over their confused heads

While time passes quickly which before felt like eternity.

◆
VIII

They sleep soundly after days

With all the emotions washing away.

He wakes up finding her gone

A sudden rush of loneliness comes his way.

◆
IX

He finds an earring on his night stand

Accompanied by a letter he reads,

Smiling wryly he packs his bags

And for the airport he calmly leaves.

◆
X

He sees the same novel again

In someone's arms as he drives the car,

He smokes his cigarette sighing bemusedly

While the red light turns green from afar.

Two wanderers in a borrowed city — one night of solace, and then the road again.
