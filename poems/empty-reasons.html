Mourning Cycle

Empty Reasons

A poem of loss, written on the spot

I

Hefty winds blow by my abode

Bringing the desolating weather

That changes direction of a door

That brings me another trice without her.

◆
II

Another spring without her mention

As I walk into my garden that does wither

I reminisce in those warm memories of affection

That has left this fallow garden, along with her.

◆
III

Another summer approaches, where her eyes

Do not throw shade on my heart that is frail,

Making me wish that this poem flies

To a place where her conscience gets to bail.

◆
IV

Another rain without the sight of the blue umbrella

That protected her from rain as she sauntered

Past the gallery, elegantly into a cappella

At the theatre without any nerves watered.

◆
V

This is to her and always her

I am pleased to where to she is heading

I wish her the best for the upcoming future,

As I continue to never cross paths with her.

◆
VI

As another spring ends without her mention,

I write to commit a poetic treason

As she still carelessly without contention

Cuts through my frail heart and its reason.

A meditation on seasonal absence and enduring loss.
