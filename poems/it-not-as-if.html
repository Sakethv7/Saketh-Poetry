It's Not As If

The confession hidden in denial

I

It's not as if I wandered my office to get a glimpse of you,

Walking the hallway while nervously fixing my watch.

It's not as if I anxiously waited to talk to you,

Plotting ways to somehow see you again.

◆
II

It's not as if I saw you in the books I read,

Or in the garden with the pretty sunflowers that resemble your smile.

It's not as if I bought you that gift you liked,

After consulting everyone who knew me.

◆
III

It's not as if something burned inside me,

When I saw you leaving the town.

It's not as if I lie to everyone,

When I say that I don't dream about you anymore.

◆
IV

It's not as if you've made yourself a home in my heart,

Or in my poem that mentions your blessed eyes.

I can't help but admit that this ballad is real,

As real as my heart that beats faster at your mention.

◆
V

Now an autumn reaches my doorstep

As my heart shifts from blue to autumn's orange

I remain hopeful for the next day.

Denial as the truest confession.
