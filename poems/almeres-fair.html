Almere's Fair

A bookstore by a lake — and loves that pass through, season after season.

I

Are you going to the Almere's fair?

Where the wind blows beside a field

With junipers, that rest by my lair,

As I write a poem of sorrow that I yield.

◆
II

I open my bookstore every season

Here in this crowded fair by a lake

Where travellers without any reason

Stumble upon books they forsake.

◆
III

I reminisce the day I failed

To express love in my poem,

A love which wasn't conveyed

In words, that weren't in tandem.

◆
IV

My heart remains heavy

With regret, sorrow and grief

As I walk by this field of Daisy

And Alpine that shower relief.

◆

Will you be there, my first love?

◆
V

Eating candy near a willow tree

Just like when we were thirteen,

Smiling with innocence and glee

As I stood there, with a hot caffeine.

◆

Will you pass by too, my second love?

◆
VI

Strolling by the lake in a blue dress,

In which I saw you for the first time

When my mind was in distress,

As my heart had felt pain on its dime.

◆

Will you saunter through the fair, my nostalgic love?

◆
VII

Would I find those eyes again

That I seemed to have forgotten,

While my heart braces for pain,

As it doubts the flowers that had fallen.

◆
VIII

Even in this fair's crowd, my alone

Eyes search for your long hair

And your eyes, as your cologne

Still lingers in the Spring's air.

◆

Will a new love pass through my bookstore?

◆
IX

Would my heart be paranoid

To be vulnerable over a love

That may find its place in a void

Inside my heart, that awaits a dove.

◆
X

Upon meeting her would I ever

Write again, as only the sorrow

In my lonely heart helps me lever

My hands to write for a tomorrow.

◆
XI

Will her eyes tell me a story

That is unique, making my heart

Light yet undoubtedly heavy

Unless this rigid fate plays its part.

◆
XII

Its a new season again under

This old willow tree's shade,

And it rains outside while I cover,

The books that just are a facade,

◆
XIII

For the flowers that often bloom

Just to dare the omni season,

Which is love, that brings doom

To the lover's heart, as a treason.

◆
XIV

So are you going to the Almere's fair?

"Will a new love pass through my bookstore?" — the whole anthology in one question.
