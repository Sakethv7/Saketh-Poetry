Mourning Cycle

The Girl with a Cat Named Whiskey

Sugarless coffee and fading warmth

I

This cold evening makes me reminisce

A time with her cat and her wry smile

Filling my hands with warmth and bliss

And memories of old now fade.

◆
II

My book of poetry drenched in blue

Remains a melancholy that hinders

My wait for a love that has been due

For countless lonely winters.

◆
III

On a cold December night she had caught my attention

When flowers were nowhere to be seen

Now a spring arrives without her mention

And flowers in the garden seem routine.

◆
IV

The cat drew me towards her in mystery

I was not fond of cats which were furry

And especially not one named whiskey

But the heart yearned change in a hurry.

◆
V

I recall sitting in her patio listening to her with sugarless coffee

Prattling about life while whiskey would sit on my jacket

Listening to our conversations as a hobby

And we would kiss, which had become a habit.

◆
VI

Nowadays I make my coffee with less sugar

I drink it with a cigarette in my hand

While I try to put a name on this feeling

That blew away with the wind as planned.

Where a cat and coffee become the only witnesses.
